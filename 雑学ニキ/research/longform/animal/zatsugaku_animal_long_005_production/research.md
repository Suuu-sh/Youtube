## Facts and source notes
- ウォンバット: Cube-shaped feces are formed in the intestine; variable stiffness and drying shape the corners; likely helps scent-marking feces stay put. Source: https://www.australiangeographic.com.au/news/2021/02/why-do-wombats-have-cube-shaped-poo-scientists-may-have-solved-the-mystery/
- レッサーパンダ: Has a pseudo-thumb from a modified wrist bone used to grasp bamboo; flexible ankles help headfirst descent. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/red-panda
- ホッキョクグマ: Fur appears white because hollow transparent hairs scatter light; black skin and insulation help retain heat. Source: https://www.britannica.com/science/Why-Are-Polar-Bears-White
- ウーパールーパー: Can regenerate limbs and multiple organs/tissues including parts of brain/spine/heart/lungs/jaws. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/axolotl
- ミーアキャット: Dark eye patches reduce sun glare; long horizontal pupils and eye membrane/closable ears support desert life. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/meerkat
- オオアリクイ: No teeth; long sticky tongue up to about 2 feet and backward spines collect ants/termites. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/giant-anteater
- フェネック: Large ears dissipate desert heat and help hear prey; furred feet protect from hot ground. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/fennec-fox
- マレーグマ: Long tongue reaches honey/insects; chest patch is sun-like and individual-looking. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/sun-bear
- ハリモグラ: No teeth; electro-sensitive snout and sticky tongue; lays egg as a mammal. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/echidna
- コモドドラゴン: Largest living lizard; young live in trees; saliva/venom role should be softened as research/interpretation ongoing. Source: https://animals.sandiegozoo.org/animals/komodo-dragon
